I am a Lecturer in the School of Earth, Atmosphere and Environment at Monash University. My research focuses on polar remote sensing, with particular emphasis on sea ice and snow in a changing climate.

Measure and Understand Sea Ice.

We bring together Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR), interferometric SAR, altimetry, machine learning, and geophysical modeling to investigate sea-ice and snow properties.

My work connects Earth observation, remote-sensing technology, oceanography, and cryosphere science.

I welcome Honours, Master’s, and PhD projects that combine satellite remote-sensing algorithms and data analysis to improve understanding of environmental change, especially from students with backgrounds in engineering, Earth science, coding, and data analysis.

Research Topic

Satellite radar observations become interpretable sea ice information.

M

Measure the sea-ice surface elevation, roughness, and volume structure.

U

Understand the response of SAR signals to snow and ice regime.

SIC

Connect sea ice knowledge to climate monitoring and operational datasets.
